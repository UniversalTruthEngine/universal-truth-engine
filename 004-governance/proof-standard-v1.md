# Proof Standard v1

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum proof and evidence expectations for Core Truths within the Universal Truth Engine.

UTE should not merely store claims. It should preserve the reasoning, evidence, derivation, and reconstruction pathway that allow a truth to be independently understood and tested.

---

## 1. Required Proof Layers

Every Core Truth should eventually expose the following proof layers.

### 1.1 Summary Proof

A short explanation of why the claim is accepted.

### 1.2 Plain-Language Explanation

A human-readable explanation suitable for a non-specialist but careful reader.

### 1.3 Step-by-Step Derivation

A structured derivation showing how the conclusion follows from prior truths, observations, or operations.

### 1.4 Formal or Symbolic Proof

Where applicable, the proof should include mathematical, logical, or symbolic form.

### 1.5 Empirical Evidence

For scientific truths, the entry should include reproducible observation, experiment, or measurement evidence.

### 1.6 Reconstruction Method

A method by which a future intelligence could rediscover or validate the truth from minimal assumptions.

### 1.7 Conditions of Validity

The domain in which the proof applies must be stated.

### 1.8 Failure Cases

The entry should state where the claim does not apply, becomes approximate, or requires a different model.

---

## 2. Mathematical Truths

Mathematical truths should prioritise:

- definitions,
- axioms,
- derivation,
- proof steps,
- dependency trace,
- and formal consistency.

Example:

```text
a² + b² = c²
```

is valid for right triangles in Euclidean geometry.

The proof must state the Euclidean condition.

---

## 3. Scientific Truths

Scientific truths should prioritise:

- reproducible evidence,
- experimental design,
- measurement method,
- uncertainty,
- domain of validity,
- and known limitations.

Scientific truths may have high confidence while still remaining open to refinement.

---

## 4. Reconstruction Requirement

Every Core Truth should ask:

```text
Could a future intelligence rediscover this truth without trusting our authority?
```

If not, the proof layer is incomplete.

---

## 5. Proof Versioning

Proofs may improve over time.

When a proof is revised, the review history should record:

- date,
- reason for revision,
- nature of change,
- reviewer or contributor,
- and whether the claim itself changed.

---

## 6. Minimum Admission Requirement

A proposed Core Truth should not enter the Core Vault unless it includes at least:

1. claim,
2. confidence level,
3. conditions of validity,
4. proof or evidence summary,
5. dependencies,
6. reconstruction method,
7. known limitations or failure cases.

---

## 7. Final Principle

A truth without an inspectable justification is fragile.

UTE should preserve not only what is believed, but why it is believed.
