# Contribution and Review Process v1

## Purpose

This document defines an initial process for proposing, reviewing, and updating Core Truths.

UTE should be expandable, but not casually editable.

Its integrity depends on disciplined admission, review, versioning, and correction.

---

## 1. Proposal Stage

A proposed Core Truth should include:

1. proposed title,
2. claim,
3. domain,
4. confidence level,
5. proof or evidence,
6. dependencies,
7. reconstruction method,
8. failure cases,
9. machine-readable metadata,
10. reason for inclusion.

---

## 2. Initial Screening

A proposal should first be checked against:

- Core Truth Standard v1,
- Proof Standard v1,
- Dependency Standard v1,
- Confidence and Uncertainty Standard v1.

---

## 3. Review Questions

Reviewers should ask:

1. Is this mathematical, logical, scientific, measurement-based, or technically reconstructive?
2. Is it foundational or merely factual?
3. Is the proof sufficient?
4. Are validity conditions explicit?
5. Are dependencies minimal and correct?
6. Is uncertainty represented honestly?
7. Does the entry improve reconstruction capability?
8. Is the machine-readable layer valid?

---

## 4. Admission Outcomes

A proposed truth may be:

- accepted,
- rejected,
- deferred,
- moved to a specialised truth engine,
- or returned for revision.

---

## 5. Revision Process

Substantive changes should update:

- review-history.md,
- machine-readable metadata,
- proof files,
- dependency graph,
- and map data if topology changes.

---

## 6. AI-Assisted Review

AI systems may assist with:

- draft generation,
- dependency checks,
- proof expansion,
- contradiction search,
- schema validation,
- and topology analysis.

AI output should not be treated as final authority without review.

---

## 7. External Feedback

External feedback should be preserved when it meaningfully improves:

- clarity,
- proof quality,
- governance,
- scalability,
- or topology.

---

## 8. Final Principle

UTE should grow carefully.

The goal is not maximum content speed, but durable epistemic quality.
