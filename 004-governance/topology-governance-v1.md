# Topology Governance v1

## Purpose

This document defines early governance principles for the UTE truth topology and visual knowledge cosmos.

The topology is not merely a visual layer. It encodes dependency, centrality, bridge relationships, and reconstruction paths.

---

## 1. Nodes

Each Core Truth is represented as a node.

A node should include:

- stable ID,
- title,
- domain,
- confidence level,
- dependency centrality,
- coordinates,
- and links to proof objects.

---

## 2. Edges

Edges represent relationships between truths.

The primary edge type is:

```text
depends_on
```

Other types may include:

- supports,
- enables,
- generalises,
- specialises,
- limits,
- analogous_to.

---

## 3. Node Size

Node size should primarily represent dependency centrality.

A truth that many other truths depend on should appear more massive.

This supports the UTE cosmological metaphor of epistemic gravity.

---

## 4. Coordinates

UTE distinguishes between:

### Stable Coordinates

Used for long-term navigation.

### Dynamic Coordinates

Generated by layout systems and allowed to shift as the graph grows.

A truth's identity is its stable ID, not its location.

---

## 5. Regions

Regions may represent major domains such as:

- Logic,
- Arithmetic,
- Geometry,
- Measurement,
- Physics,
- Chemistry,
- Biology,
- Computation.

Regions should help navigation, not impose false rigidity.

---

## 6. Dependency Highlighting

The map should allow users to see:

- direct dependencies,
- recursive dependencies,
- depended-on-by relationships,
- bridge truths,
- and reconstruction paths.

---

## 7. Future Topology Intelligence

The topology may later support:

- missing node detection,
- contradiction detection,
- centrality analysis,
- bridge-node analysis,
- shortest reconstruction paths,
- and topology health checks.

---

## 8. Final Principle

The map should not merely illustrate UTE.

The map should help reveal the structure of knowledge itself.
