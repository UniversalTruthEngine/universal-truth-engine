# UTE-FV-0103 — Subtraction Proof

## Claim

Subtraction determines the remaining quantity or difference when one quantity is removed from or compared with another.

## Plain-Language Explanation

If a collection has five items and two are removed, three remain.

```text
5 - 2 = 3
```

Subtraction can also express difference:

```text
5 is 2 more than 3
```

## Dependencies

- UTE-FV-0002 — Equality of Quantity
- UTE-FV-0003 — Counting and Natural Numbers
- UTE-FV-0100 — Number
- UTE-FV-0101 — Natural Number
- UTE-FV-0102 — Addition

## Reconstruction Method

A future intelligence can rediscover subtraction by:
1. starting with a counted collection,
2. removing a counted subcollection,
3. counting what remains,
4. or comparing two quantities to identify their difference.

## Conditions of Validity

The quantities must be defined within a compatible context.

## Failure Cases

Subtraction is misused if removed items are not part of the original collection, if units are incompatible, or if the comparison context is unclear.

## UTE Importance

Subtraction supports arithmetic, comparison, balance, inverse operations, algebra, and measurement.
