# UTE-FV-0101 — Natural Number Proof

## Claim

A natural number is a member of the ordered counting sequence used to represent discrete quantity.

## Plain-Language Explanation

Natural numbers arise from counting distinguishable items.

The sequence:

```text
1, 2, 3, 4, ...
```

represents repeated successor steps.

## Dependencies

- UTE-FV-0003 — Counting and Natural Numbers
- UTE-FV-0100 — Number

## Reconstruction Method

A future intelligence can rediscover natural numbers by assigning one ordered marker to each item in a stable collection.

## Conditions of Validity

Natural numbers apply most directly to discrete countable quantities.

## Failure Cases

They may be misapplied when continuous measurement, approximation, or ambiguous grouping is treated as simple counting.

## UTE Importance

Natural numbers support arithmetic operations including addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
