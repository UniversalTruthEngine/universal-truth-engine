# Core vs Stable Distinction

## Purpose

UTE distinguishes between two different properties:

- **Core**: how structurally central a truth is.
- **Stable**: how strongly validated or durable the truth is.

These are related but not identical.

---

## Core

A truth is core when many other truths depend on it or when it has high reconstructive leverage.

Examples:

- Equality
- Counting
- Addition
- Logical implication
- Measurement

---

## Stable

A truth is stable when its confidence level is high and its conditions of validity are well understood.

Examples:

- Arithmetic identities
- Pythagorean theorem within Euclidean geometry
- Conservation laws within known domains

---

## Why This Matters

A truth can be:

| Truth | Core? | Stable? |
|---|---|---|
| Equality | Very core | Very stable |
| Newtonian mechanics | Moderately core | Stable within domain |
| Semiconductor lithography | Less core | Stable but highly dependent |
| Economic forecasting model | Less core | Less stable |

---

## Rule

Do not confuse foundational importance with certainty.

A truth may be important and still require clear conditions, limitations, and review.
