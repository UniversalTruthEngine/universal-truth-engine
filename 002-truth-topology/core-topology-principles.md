# Core Topology Principles

## Purpose

The Truth Topology is the navigable relationship layer of the Universal Truth Engine.

It is intended to support:

- human learning,
- AI traversal,
- dependency analysis,
- reconstruction sequencing,
- and eventually a visual cosmological map of fundamental knowledge.

---

## Principle 1 — Truths Are Nodes

Each Core Truth is a node in the topology.

A node should have:

- stable ID,
- title,
- category,
- confidence level,
- dependency links,
- reconstruction difficulty,
- and civilisational leverage.

---

## Principle 2 — Relationships Are Edges

Truths become useful when their relationships are explicit.

Edges may represent:

- dependency,
- derivation,
- support,
- analogy,
- limitation,
- or generalisation.

---

## Principle 3 — Core Does Not Mean Final

A Core Truth is structurally important.

It is not necessarily absolute, dogmatic, or immune from revision.

Core status reflects:

- dependency density,
- foundational value,
- reconstructive importance,
- and epistemic leverage.

---

## Principle 4 — The Map Should Be Navigable

The eventual UTE map should be usable as:

- a dependency graph,
- an educational path,
- a civilisational bootstrap sequence,
- a machine-readable reasoning substrate,
- and a visual knowledge cosmos.

---

## Principle 5 — Distance Should Have Meaning

In future visualisations, distance between nodes may represent a weighted combination of:

- dependency strength,
- semantic similarity,
- abstraction level,
- reconstruction order,
- confidence,
- and cross-domain relevance.

The visual 3D map should be a projection of deeper graph structure, not an arbitrary illustration.

---

## Principle 6 — Missing Nodes Matter

A well-formed topology should help reveal missing truths.

If two regions require a bridge truth not yet defined, the topology should make that gap visible.

This is one of the major reasons UTE should be useful to future AI systems.

---

## Principle 7 — Human and AI Layers Should Coexist

The topology should remain:

- readable by humans,
- parseable by machines,
- navigable visually,
- and recoverable from plain text.

UTE should not depend on a single fragile interface.
