# Admission Standards

A truth may be admitted into the Core Vault if it is:

1. clearly stated,
2. assigned a confidence level,
3. linked to dependencies,
4. accompanied by proof, evidence, or reconstruction pathway,
5. explicit about failure cases,
6. useful for future reasoning or reconstruction,
7. interpretable by humans and machines.

## Vault Tiers

- Core Vault: durable, foundational, high-confidence truths
- Extended Vault: useful but less foundational truths
- Speculative Annex: hypotheses, disputed claims, incomplete models
- Historical Annex: influential but superseded or false claims
