# Fact Vault Charter v1

## Purpose

The Fact Vault exists to preserve highly durable, verifiable knowledge that can:

- survive technological or institutional disruption,
- be independently re-derived,
- assist future reconstruction of scientific and technical civilisation,
- and preserve reliable methods of reasoning, not merely raw information.

The Vault prioritises:

1. Reproducibility
2. Clarity
3. Independence from fragile infrastructure
4. Minimal assumptions
5. Long-term interpretability

## Core Principles

### Facts are graded, not absolute

Every entry must include confidence level, assumptions, applicable conditions, known limitations, and dependency chains.

### Re-derivability matters more than authority

A fact should ideally be reproducible from direct observation, mathematics, experiment, or independently repeatable procedures.

### Simplicity is preferred

If two explanations are equally accurate, the simpler and more reconstructable version should be preserved.

### Preserve reasoning, not only conclusions

Every important fact should include derivation, proof, experimental basis, or reasoning pathway.

### Transparency over certainty

Unknowns, disputes, and uncertainty must be explicit.
