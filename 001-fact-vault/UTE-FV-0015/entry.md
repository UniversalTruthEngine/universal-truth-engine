# UTE-FV-0015 — Law of Identity

## Statement

A defined thing, symbol, or reference is identical to itself within a stable context.

## Domain

Logic

## Dependencies

None.

## Summary

The Law of Identity states that a thing is itself.

In symbolic form:

```text
X = X
```

For UTE purposes, this means that a defined reference must preserve itself within a reasoning context unless explicitly redefined.

## Boundary Notes

The Law of Identity does not claim that different things are the same.

It does not by itself solve questions about:
- change over time,
- transformation,
- equivalence,
- similarity,
- category membership,
- or approximate sameness.

It only states that a defined reference is self-identical within the relevant context.
