# UTE-FV-0102 — Addition Proof

## Claim

Addition combines quantities into a single total while preserving countable quantity.

## Plain-Language Explanation

If one collection has two items and another collection has three items, combining them gives a collection of five items.

```text
2 + 3 = 5
```

## Dependencies

- UTE-FV-0002 — Equality of Quantity
- UTE-FV-0003 — Counting and Natural Numbers
- UTE-FV-0100 — Number
- UTE-FV-0101 — Natural Number

## Reconstruction Method

A future intelligence can rediscover addition by:
1. forming two distinguishable collections,
2. combining them,
3. counting the combined collection,
4. recognising the combined total as the additive result.

## Conditions of Validity

The counted objects or units must be compatible within the chosen context.

## Failure Cases

Addition is misused if incompatible units are combined without conversion or if collections overlap without being accounted for.

## UTE Importance

Addition is a primary arithmetic operation and supports subtraction, multiplication, algebra, measurement, and computation.
