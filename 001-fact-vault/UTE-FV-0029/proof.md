# Coordinate System Proof

## Claim
A coordinate system provides a structured framework for locating or comparing positions relative to defined references.

## Plain-Language Explanation

A coordinate system provides a structured framework for locating or comparing positions relative to defined references.

Measurement systems depend on stable comparison structures.

This principle forms part of the deeper ontology beneath human units such as metres or seconds.

## Step-by-Step Reasoning

1. Distinguishable entities or states must exist.
2. Comparable properties must be observable.
3. Stable references must persist long enough for comparison.
4. Repeatable comparison enables consistent relation between entities.
5. Structured comparison forms the basis of measurement.

A future intelligence can rediscover this principle through repeated comparison of distinguishable states and preservation of stable references.

## Conditions of Validity

Applies within systems capable of preserving distinguishability and stable comparison.

## Failure Cases

Measurement degrades when:
- references drift uncontrollably,
- comparisons cannot be repeated,
- distinctions collapse,
- or ordering becomes unstable.
